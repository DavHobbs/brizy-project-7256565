Will Botox make me feel numb and look frozen?

You may experience an abnormal facial change shortly after a procedure is performed. These are common side effects of botox that are neither permanent nor serious. Other symptoms include vision problems (like double vision or blurred vision), dry eyes, and muscle weakness.


Depending on the planned injection site, hoarseness, tiredness, difficulty swallowing, loss of voice, and dry mouth are common side-effects, and the risk of serious side effects is low.

Does Botox hurt?

Aside from the needle injection, Botox injections are not especially painful. However, you may experience pain or swelling at the injection site, as well as a mild fever or dizziness, as a side effect. If a skin infection occurs, contact a medical professional.

How long does Botox last?

Typical doses of botox usually last three to four months. Afterward, you may consider further injection of botox. Mild side-effects, including loss of strength or swelling of your eyelids, typically die down within a few days of the procedure.
